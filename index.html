THERE IS NO ONE

By Osubula · May 2024 · 7 min read · Article 1 of 14 in Intersexual Dynamics
The belief that one person holds the key to your entire romantic future is not love — it is a trap dressed up in pretty words.

I want to start with something most people won't say out loud. There is no "The One." There never was. It is one of the most comfortable lies we have ever agreed to believe together.

What Is ONEitis?

ONEitis is when you lock your entire emotional future onto one person. You convince yourself they are irreplaceable. You start making decisions based on keeping them, not based on what is actually good for you.

I have watched men destroy themselves over this. Good men. Smart men. Men who could reason their way through a business problem in ten minutes but could not see what was happening to them in their own relationship.

It feels like love. That is the trap. It is actually fear dressed up as devotion. Fear that you are not enough to attract someone else. Fear that this was your one shot and you blew it. Fear running the whole show, and you calling it love.

The Soulmate Myth (The Fantasy Everyone Believes)

We were all taught this. Movies, songs, books, and ads have been drilling the same story into us since childhood. One day you meet THE perfect person. You just know. Everything falls into place. That is the template.

It is a beautiful story. It is also one of the most damaging ideas ever sold to men and women. It makes people waste years chasing a feeling instead of building something real. I have seen it happen. I have done it myself.

The dating industry profits from this myth directly. They need you to believe your soulmate is one more swipe away. The longer you believe that, the longer you stay on the platform. The longer you stay, the more money they make. Your loneliness is their business model.

Why It Messes People Up

When you are single and you believe in "The One," every failed date feels catastrophic. You are not just unmatched — you are failing to find your destiny. That pressure changes how you show up. You stop being curious. You start being desperate. People feel that difference immediately.

When you are already in a relationship, the myth becomes a trap you built yourself. You stay past the point you should leave. You tolerate things you should not tolerate. You ignore red flags because you have already decided this person is irreplaceable. You try to fix them. You try to fix yourself. Anything to make the fantasy work.

It Creates a Power Problem

When you decide someone is your only option, you hand them all the power. Not because they took it. Because you gave it. The moment they know — consciously or not — that you will not leave, the dynamic shifts. You stop being a partner. You become someone who is managed.

I have seen this: A woman stays with a man who humiliates her publicly. Her friends see it clearly. She sees it too. But she has convinced herself the connection is rare. So she stays. She has made a myth more important than her own dignity.
And this: A man watches his marriage fall apart slowly — contempt at the dinner table, decisions made without him, respect gone years ago. He stays because his entire identity is built around being her husband. Losing that feels worse than losing himself. So he loses himself quietly, every single day.
Where Does This Idea Come From?
  • Pop culture — every film, song, and novel repeating the same story until it feels like truth
  • Dating apps built to profit from your belief that the perfect match is just one more swipe away
  • Society, repeating the myth like religion — generation after generation, never questioning it

I have noticed something about the men who push back hardest on this. They are perfectly logical about money, career, and health. The moment you touch this myth, they get emotional and defensive. That tells you everything. The belief is not in their head. It is in their identity.

Healthy Love vs. ONEitis
✓ Healthy Relationship

Both people choose each other every day. Both can leave. Neither one is begging. That is what respect looks like.

✕ ONEitis Relationship

One person cannot imagine leaving. They stay out of fear, not choice. That is not love. That is dependency with a romantic name on it.

The Better Way to Think

Here is what I know. There are many good people out there who you could build something real with. Some will be great for you. Some will be terrible. But none of them is your only shot. Not one.

When you actually accept that, something shifts. You stop performing. You stop shrinking yourself to fit what someone else wants. You show up as you actually are. And that version of you — the unedited one — is far more attractive than anything fear could design.

Drop the myth. Not because love is not real. It is. But the version the myth is selling keeps you small, scared, and stuck. Real love is chosen. Every day. By two people who both know they could leave — and both decide to stay.

There is no One. There are many good ones. That is not sad news. That is the most freeing thing I have ever written down.

THERE IS NO ONE.
THERE ARE MANY GOOD ONES —
AND THAT'S ACTUALLY MUCH BETTER NEWS.

THE RED PILL PATH

By Osubula · June 2024 · 8 min read · Article 1 of 11 in Masculinity & Identity
The Red Pill is a lens, not a leash. It gives you clarity — what you do with that clarity is entirely up to you.

Let me be direct about what the Red Pill is and what it is not. It is not a religion. It is not a political movement. It is not a rulebook telling you how to treat women. It is a practical framework for understanding how men and women actually behave in relationships — not how we wish they behaved, not how society says they should behave. What is real. That is all.

Its Core Principles

The Red Pill tries to stay objective. It asks uncomfortable questions about male and female behaviour without flinching. When better information appears, it updates. It is written in pencil, not ink. And it gives you tools and awareness — but what you do with them is entirely your decision. Nobody is telling you what to conclude.

That last part matters. I have seen too many men treat it like scripture. They find a few ideas that resonate and suddenly they have a religion. That is the opposite of the point.

The Problem

Most people cannot encounter an idea that challenges their worldview without immediately trying to domesticate it. They take Red Pill truths and bend them to fit what they already believe. They keep what flatters them. They discard what challenges them. Then they call that thinking.

When Red Pill observations genuinely threaten someone's self-image, one of two things happens. They redefine it — they say "that is misogyny" or "that is toxic" — anything to avoid engaging with the actual content. Or they get angry. The anger is always the tell. Anger is what happens when a belief you built your life around starts to crack.

The Blue Pill runs deep. It is not just an idea you were taught. It is the story you built your identity around — about love, about fairness, about how the world owes you if you are a good person. Questioning it feels like an attack on yourself. I know. I went through it too. But once you see clearly, you cannot unsee it.

How to Approach the Red Pill

There is no formula here. No twelve steps. No guru to follow. I am not interested in disciples. The moment you start treating any thinker — including me — as someone with all the answers, you have already failed the first test.

The goal is simple. Give men accurate information. Let them think. Let them decide. Men should be the artists of their own lives, not sheep looking for a shepherd. If you leave this blog looking for someone to tell you exactly what to do next, you missed everything.

How to Use the Red Pill Correctly
  • Start with an open, neutral mind. Not a defensive one.
  • Study the data and patterns honestly. Not selectively.
  • Accept what is true, even when it is uncomfortable. Especially then.
  • Build your own practical plan from that truth. Your life, your architecture.
  • Do not use it to justify anger, bitterness, or hopelessness. That is the Blue Pill in disguise.
What It Is — And What It Isn't

The Red Pill is a tool for understanding reality. That is the whole job description. It is not your moral compass. It is not your life plan. It is not a community you join or an identity you adopt. It is a lens. You look through it, you see more clearly, and then you decide what to do with what you see.

Real growth does not come from finding the right set of beliefs to adopt. It comes from facing what is true, sitting with the discomfort of it, and then building your own path forward. Not someone else's path. Yours.

Stay objective. Stay curious. Take ownership of your life. Nobody is coming to rescue you — and that is actually the best news you will hear today.

STAY OBJECTIVE.
STAY CURIOUS.
TAKE OWNERSHIP OF YOUR LIFE.
